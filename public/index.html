Plethysmographic Waveform Reference

×
Index / Feature Description Normal / Threshold Clinical Notes
Perfusion Index (PI) AC/DC ratio ×100% — perfusion quality >1.5–2% good; <0.5% poor Low PI = vasoconstriction, poor peripheral perfusion
Pleth Variability Index (PVI) Respiratory variation in amplitude <10–15% (fluid responsive if >13–20% ventilated) Elevated PVI in ventilated patients predicts fluid responsiveness
Simple Systolic Ratio (SSR) Max / min peak amplitude ratio ~1.0–1.5; >1.5 = fluid responsive SSR >1.5 indicates significant respiratory variation
Amplitude Peak-to-trough height >2–5% of DC baseline (device-dependent) Low amplitude = hypoperfusion or signal artifact
Upstroke (Systolic) Rapid rise to systolic peak Sharp, steep Blunted upstroke = reduced cardiac output or arterial stiffness
Dicrotic Notch Small notch after systolic peak — aortic valve closure Visible in healthy adults Absent notch = vasodilation / sepsis; exaggerated = hypovolemia
Diastolic Decay Gradual fall after dicrotic notch Smooth, exponential Rapid decay = low peripheral resistance; slow = high resistance
